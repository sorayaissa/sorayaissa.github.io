Simon Jakes

Clinical Psychologist

Psychological Therapy

Simon Jakes is a clinical psychologist, based in Woonona, who works with clients with emotional disorders, longterm difficulties in relationships, difficulties in emotional control and self-harm. Clients often have a history of trauma or are undergoing current life difficulties. He has post-qualification training in CBT, psychodynamic psychotherapy and Schema therapy.

Training

Simon took his first degree in psychology and trained as a clinical psychologist at the Institute of Psychiatry in London. He did post-qualification at the Tavistock. He began working as a clinical psychologist in 1982. He worked in England until 2000 when he moved and has worked here since then.

About

Simon is based in Woonona. He took his first degree in psychology at Oxford University and trained as a clinical psychologist at the Institute of Psychiatry in London. He did post-qualification training at the Tavistock. He began working as a clinical psycologist in 1982.

He lived in England until 2000 when he moved and has worked here since then. Simon has co-authored a book on psychological therapy for psychosis with John Rhodes. He has also published a second book on psychological theories of psychosis and the implications for therapy.

Psychotherapy

Simon is interested in a wide range of psychological problems.

-emotional disorder and anxiety, panic and depression.

-longterm difficulties in relationships

-difficulties in emotional control and self-harm

-Trauma

-Difficulty in coping with current problems.

He uses an individualised, eclectic approach to therapy drawing on therapies which have a solid base in the literature and also take into account the preferences and needs of the client.

Interests

He is married with children. He is a member of the clinical college of the APS and the clinical section of the British Psychological Society. He is undergoing training in Schema Therapy.
